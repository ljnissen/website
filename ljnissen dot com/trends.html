THE TREND IS YOUR FRIEND

Today I will take a look at the basics of technical analysis.

Even if the last week of 2014 was a red week (a negative week), it doesn't change the fact that the S&P500 still was up 11.7% for the year.

If you as I look at the intermediate movements in the market, you will see that both the 50-week and 200-week moving averages are moving upwards (Fig. 1).

This is positive.

Another indicator that is worth taking a look at is the Average Directional Movement Index (ADX). The indicator divides price action of recent days into two distinct directional indicators (+DI and -DI). When they cross each other the upward (or downward) trends are gaining momentum.

This does not paint an as rosy picture.

The ADX turned negative on Friday the 14'th of December and has been moving down since (Fig. 2).

So where does this leave us?

The long term trend is still up as shown by the rising moving averages. However, in the shorter time-frame the index may take a hit.

So it may be a good idea to wait until the S&P500 comes down to kiss the 50-week moving average before putting money at play.
